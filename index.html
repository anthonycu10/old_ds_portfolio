Education

With over 7 years experience of tutoring elementary, middle, high school, and college students, both in-person and online, I'm complementing my double major with a minor in education with a focus on the secondary track of science and mathematics.

I currently work as an Undergraduate Learning Assistant (ULA) for the Department of Statistics and Applied Probability, in which I hold biweekly office hours, lead lab sections, and facilitate lectures alongside faculty to answer student questions. As a ULA for PSTAT 10: Principles of Data Science with R for over 5 quarters now, I’ve had the opportunity to add my own insights into the course in shaping and curating course content. Some of my proudest accomplishments as a ULA have been:

  • hosting a finals review session during dead week, in which I prepared PowerPoint slides and a review guide, ultimatley yielding a turnout of 50+ students in attendance
  • creating a quiz bank database with over 7 quarters worth of past material that would be utilized in future quarters
  • closely working with first-time coders in learning R; by encouraging and showing my care in their academic journeys, I motivated them to persevere throughout all the coding errors we faced

As I strive to facilitate my students’ learning, my greatest priority as an instructor is to build rapport: I believe that a student is best able to learn in an environment where they feel comfortable enough to ask questions and make mistakes. My consistent efforts in building this rapport have successfully aided me in transforming the tutor-tutee relationship into friendships, ultimately encouraging students to attend drop-in hours more regularly and become more proactive with their education.
